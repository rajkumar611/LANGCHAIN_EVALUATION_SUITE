ACME CORP — PROJECT ATLAS: INITIATIVE CLOSURE REPORT
=====================================================

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------
This report documents the discontinuation of Project Atlas, an internal platform modernisation initiative, and summarises the unrecovered capital expenditure incurred prior to closure.

CLOSURE DECISION AND RATIONALE
--------------------------------
Following a board review in Q3, Project Atlas was formally discontinued due to a combination of scope misalignment and vendor non-delivery. The initiative had been running for 14 months with a remit to replace the company's legacy data warehouse. The decision to terminate the initiative was unanimous among senior leadership.

The primary drivers behind the discontinuation were:
- The selected vendor failed to deliver the core integration module within the agreed timeline
- Internal resource allocation shifted following a company-wide restructuring
- A revised technology strategy made the original scope redundant

FINANCIAL IMPACT
----------------
The total unrecovered capital expenditure attributable to Project Atlas is $1,340,000. This figure encompasses vendor licensing fees paid upfront ($620,000), internal staff costs allocated to the initiative ($480,000), and third-party consulting fees ($240,000). None of these costs are recoverable following the closure decision.

The budget variance against the original approved spend of $900,000 represents an overrun of $440,000, driven primarily by extended consulting engagement required to manage vendor remediation efforts.

LESSONS LEARNED
---------------
The primary lesson from this initiative is the importance of milestone-based payment structures with external vendors. Future initiatives of this scale will require contractual performance gates before significant capital is released.

TOTAL FINANCIAL LOSS FROM CANCELLATION: $1,340,000
PROJECT STATUS: Discontinued (Q3)
ROOT CAUSE: Vendor non-delivery and strategic misalignment
