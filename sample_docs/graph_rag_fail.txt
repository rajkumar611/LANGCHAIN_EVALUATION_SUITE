NEXUS CORP — KUBERNETES UPGRADE FAQ
=====================================

Quick answers for engineers completing the Kubernetes v1.28 to v1.29.2 upgrade.

Q: Why do we need to upgrade Kubernetes?
A: CVE-2024-7891 is a critical privilege escalation vulnerability in Kubernetes v1.26–v1.28 with a public exploit. Upgrading to v1.29.2 removes the vulnerability. Deadline is 72 hours.

Q: Who needs to act?
A: Platform Team and AI Research Team. Both use Kubernetes v1.28 in production. Security Team (Marcus Rodriguez) will confirm completion.

Q: Which projects are affected?
A: Project Nova, Project Atlas, and Project Meridian all use Kubernetes v1.28. All three must be validated after the upgrade.

Q: How long will the upgrade take?
A: The rolling cluster restart takes approximately 20–30 minutes. Coordinate with your team manager to schedule during a low-traffic window to minimise request drops.

Q: What is the upgrade command?
A: Follow the runbook provided by the Security Team. Do not deviate from the steps without approval from Marcus Rodriguez.

Q: What do I do after the upgrade?
A: Run the Security Team's automated validation suite. File your compliance confirmation within 48 hours of completing the upgrade. Non-compliance will be escalated to the CTO.

Q: What if I find a blocker?
A: Contact Nina Okafor or Tom Brennan on the Security Team immediately. Do not delay — the 72-hour deadline is firm.

Q: Is there any risk of downtime?
A: Moderate risk. Project Nova inference endpoints may drop requests for 2–5 minutes during the rolling update. Plan accordingly.
